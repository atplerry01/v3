# WHYCESPACE WBSM v3
# HEOS — HUMAN ECONOMIC OPERATING SYSTEM
# 2.1.17 ROLE ENGINE

You are implementing the **Role Engine** for HEOS (Human Economic Operating System).

The Role Engine manages **assignment and removal of participant roles** within the Whycespace ecosystem.

Roles determine the operational classification of a participant.

Participants may hold roles such as:

• Participant  
• WorkforceMember  
• Operator  
• Investor  
• Administrator  

The Role Engine operates on the **Participant Aggregate** defined in:

src/domain/core/participants/

This implementation must follow **WBSM v3 Prompt Structural Rules and Guardrails**.

---

# STEP 1 — ARCHITECTURE VERIFICATION

Component classification:

Role Engine = **Execution Engine**

Correct layer:

src/engines/T2E/

Reason:

Role management is deterministic business logic applied to domain aggregates.

The engine performs business decisions but does not orchestrate workflows.

Therefore it belongs in **T2E Execution Engines**.

---

# STEP 7 — ROLE RULES

The engine must enforce:

A participant cannot have duplicate roles.

Administrator role may only be assigned through privileged workflow.

Operator role requires participant status = Active.

Removing the final role is not allowed.

Suspended participants cannot receive new roles.

---

# STEP 8 — ENGINE INVOCATION RULE

The Role Engine must never call another engine.

Correct invocation:

Runtime Dispatcher → RoleEngine

Incorrect:

RoleEngine → another engine

Runtime orchestration must invoke engines.
