## Component Details

- **Component Number:** 2.2.23

- **System Category:** Capital System
- **Architecture Layer:** Engine Layer
- **Engine Tier:** T3I — Intelligence Engine
- **Component Category:** Intelligence Engine

## Purpose

The Capital Validation Engine verifies the integrity and validity of capital operations before execution. It ensures all capital lifecycle transitions comply with capital pool constraints, reservation availability, allocation limits, investor participation rules, and system financial integrity rules.

## Implementation

- **Engine:** `src/engines/T3I/economic/capital/CapitalValidationEngine.cs`
- **Command:** `src/engines/T3I/economic/capital/ValidateCapitalOperationCommand.cs`
- **Result:** `src/engines/T3I/economic/capital/CapitalValidationResult.cs`
- **Tests:** `tests/capital-system/CapitalValidationEngineTests.cs`

## Validation Rules

- Pool exists and is active
- Contribution amount valid (positive)
- Reservation amount within pool available balance
- Reservation must exist before allocation
- Allocation must exist before utilization
- Investor authorized to participate
- Contribution within allowed limits
- Currency consistency across pool operations

## Operation Types

- Commitment
- Contribution
- Reservation
- Allocation
- Utilization
- Distribution

## Validation Error Codes

- POOL_NOT_FOUND
- POOL_NOT_ACTIVE
- INSUFFICIENT_POOL_CAPITAL
- INVALID_RESERVATION
- RESERVATION_NOT_ACTIVE
- INVALID_ALLOCATION_REFERENCE
- INVALID_LIFECYCLE_STATE
- INVESTOR_NOT_FOUND
- INVESTOR_NOT_AUTHORIZED
- INVESTOR_LIMIT_EXCEEDED
- INVALID_AMOUNT
- UNSUPPORTED_CURRENCY
- CURRENCY_MISMATCH

## Constraints

- Stateless, deterministic, thread-safe
- No persistence, no event emission, no engine-to-engine calls
- Returns immutable validation results only
